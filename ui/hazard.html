Hazardous Foods

Alcohol

Alcoholic drinks can cause poisoning to your dog. Alcohol is also a major by-product of ingested yeast dough. Small amounts of alcohol, especially in small dogs can be life-threatening toxicity.
Symptoms:
  • Drooling
  • Vomiting
  • Weakness
  • Neurological depression
  • Hypothermia
  • Hypotension
  • Seizures
  • Respiratory failure
  • Death

Avocado

Avocado leaves, fruit, seeds, and bark may contain a toxic principle know as persin. The most problematic appears to be the Guatemalan avocados that are more commonly found in stores. If your dog eats avocado there will not be serious signs of illness.
Symptoms:
  • Mild stomach upset may occur if your dog eats significant amount of avocado.

Warning: Ingestion of the pit can lead to obstruction in the gastrointestinal tract, which is a serious hazard that requires urgent veterinary care.

Baby Food

Baby food may be healthy for human babies, however, it is not the same for puppies and dogs. Although most baby food are not really that toxic to dogs, these type of foods are not the proper nutrition. There are certain ingredients that are not suitable to the dog's digestive system. It can make your dog a picky eater as well as having bad ingredients. Some baby food contains grape, which may be toxic to dogs. Garlic and onion can upset your dog's stomach.

Symptoms may vary depending on the ingredients inside baby foods.

Cooked Bones

Certain types of bones such as cooked bones are brittle and splinter. They can also break teeth and cause enamel damange. Due to the splinter, the bones damage the stomach and intestine. Raw bones don't splinter, but may contain bacteria that cause diarrhea and illness.
Symptoms
  • Intestinal blockage
Warning: Work with your veterinarian to determine what is the best for your dog.

Chocolate

Chocolate has compounds that causes toxicosis are caffeine and theobromine, which belongs to the group of chemicals called methylxanthines. Dark chocolate contains high levels of methylxanthine. Chocolate are very harmful to your dog.
Symptoms:
  • Vomiting
  • Increased thirst
  • Abdominal discomfort
  • Restlessness
  • Severe agitation
  • Muscle tremors
  • Irregular heart rhythm
  • High body temperature
  • Seizures
  • Death

Warning: If your dog shows more than mild restlessness, your dog should be taken the veterinarian immediately.

Citrus Fruits

Citrus fruits such as lemon, lime, orange, grapefruit, and other fruits are toxic to your dog. There are poisonous components such as psoralen compounds and aromatic oils. These citrus fruits have high citric acid level.
Symptoms
  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Upset stomach
Warning: If your dog has been eating large amounts of fruits, issues with the central nervous system may arise. If there are concerns with unusual behaviour and reaction, notify your veterinarian immediately.

Coffee

Coffee or coffee grounds contain caffeine which is toxic to dogs. Ingestion of moderate amount can easily cause death in small dogs.
Symptoms
  • Severe hyperactivity
  • Restlessness
  • Vomiting
  • Abnormal heart rhythms
  • Seizures
  • Collapse
Warning: If you suspect your dog ingesting any type of caffeine products, please take you dog to the veterinarian care immediately.

Corn

Corn on its own, is not toxic or generally harmful to your dogs. The main issue with eating corn on the cob is that some dogs may swallow the whole cob or a large chunk of it. Generally, it is often most difficult to pass because of its size. It may get stuck somewhere within the dog's digestive tract causing partial or complete intestinal obstruction.
Symptoms
  • Vomiting
  • Straining during defecation
  • Loss of appetite
  • Stomach pain
  • Whimpering
  • Restlessness
Warning: If you see any of the symptoms, see your veterinary immediately.

Fish

Fish are not poisonous to dogs, however, the parasites in fish are. If you deciede to feed fish for your dog, WebMD advises to cook the fish thoroughly to kill any parasites. Raw fish can contain parasites that can make your dog sick with salmonpoisoning. The fish bones are also choking hazards and can cause digestive tract complication.
Symptoms: Within six days of dog eating an infected fish would be
  • Vomiting
  • Lack of appetite
  • Fever
  • Diarrhea
  • Wweakness
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Dehydration
Warning: Parasites that are present in fish can be extremely dangerous and can have fatal results within 14 days of infection. Call your veterinary immediately.

Grapes

Grapes and Raisins have been associated with the development of kidney failure. Some dogs eat this fruit without harm while others have develop life-threatening problems after eating a few grapes or raisins. It is not clear on why some dog can eat grapes or raisins without harm and others become life threatening. The safest course of action is to not feed your dog grapes or raisins.

Symptoms: Dogs experiencing grapes or raisin toxicosis usually develop vomiting and diarrhea within 12 hours of ingestions. As signs progress, dog become dehydrated and may refuse to eat, show an increase in urination. Following later stages, signs may show of decreased or absent urination. Death may show within three to four days, or long-term kidney disease may persist in the dogs that survive the acute intoxication.
Warning: Successful treatment requires prompt veterinary treatment to maintain good urine flow.

Gum

Gum contains xylitol which is toxic to dogs. The ingestion of xylitol or by-products causes a rapid realease of hormone insulin, causing a sudden decrease in the dog's blood glucose.
Symptoms
  • Lethargy
  • Vomiting
  • Loss of coordination
  • Collapse
  • Seizures

Warning: Liver failure may occur in severe cases of toxicity due to very low blood sugar. See your veterinarian immediately if symptoms are apparent.

Hops

Hops is a plant used in the process of brewing beer. The exact toxic is unknown but may be related to essential oils, resins, phenolic compounds, or nitrogen within the plant.
Symptoms
  • High body temperature
  • Increased breathing
  • High heart rate
  • Vomiting
  • Abnormal clotting
  • Death
Warning: Death has been reported in dogs poisoned by hops within 6 hours of ingestion if not been treated. Please see your veterinary immediately.

Macadamia Nuts

Macadamia nut toxicosis is not fatal in dogs, however it can cause your dog to become very uncomfortable.
Symptoms:
  • Weakness in the rear legs
  • Appear to be in pain
  • Tremors
  • Develop a low fever
These signs should subside over 48 hours.
Warning: If your dog is experiencing more mild symptoms, visit the veterinary care to ease the pain, which includes intravenous fluid therapy and pain control.

Milk

Milk may not be very harmful to your dog, however, like humans, can suffer from lactose intolerance. Dogs can drink milk, but not in excessive amounts can cause digestive problems.
Symptoms
  • Gassiness
  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting

Onion

Onion contain compounds that can damange dogs' red blood cells if ingested in sufficient amounts. The damage to the red blood cells caused by onion.
Symptoms become apparent until three to five days after ingestion.
  • Weakness
  • Appear to be tired easily
  • Orange-tinged to dark red urine
Warning: If symptoms occur, see veterinarian immediately. In severe cases, blood transfusion may be needed.

Tobacco

Consuming discarded tobacco or nicotine product can be dangerous to pets. Nicotine causes illness and even death in fairly small amounts. The nicotine toxicity varies by the size of your dog.

Yeast

Raw bread dough with live yeast can be hazardous. If ingested, the raw dough will create an ideal environment for yeast to multiple being that the stomach is warm and moist. This will result in an expansion of the dough inside the stomach. This may be severe enough to cause decreased blood flow to the stomach wall, resulting in the death of tissue. This also will cause the expansion of the stomach to push on the diaphragm, making it difficult to breath. When yeast multiplies it produces alcohols that can be absorbed and lead to alcohol intoxication.
Symptoms:
  • May have distended abdomens
  • Show lack of coordination
  • Disorientation
  • Stupor
  • Vomiting
Extreme symptoms includes coma or seizures and could lead to death from alcohol intoxication.
Warning: If your dog is showing mild signs such as severe abdominal distension or inebriated that they can't stand up should be taken to the veterinarian until they recover.
