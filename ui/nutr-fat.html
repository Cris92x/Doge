Fats and Oils


Fats and oils should provide about 10-15% fat in a healthy diet. The time when fats and oil in a diet is harmful is when they are allowed to eat excessive fat and calories without proper exercise to balance things out. Fats provide energy, makes food tastier, promote a healthy heart, and keep your dog's coat healthy and shiny.

  • Essential fatty acids are divided into two groups called omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids. Fat are important components in the production of hormone-like substances called prostaglandins which works to reduce inflammation. Fats and oils give structure to foods and help the body absorb certain vitamins such as A, D, E, and K.
  • Avoid lower-quality ingredients such as beef tallow or lard.
  • Look for where the fats and oils are coming from in the list of ingredients. Fats in dog food are supplied by animal fat and oils from plants. Omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids are usually listed in quality dog foods. These fatty acids help promote heart health, skin and coat, and optimal brain function.

Fish oils such as herring or salmon and flaxseed and canola oils are sources of omega-3 fatty acids. Pork fat, chicken fat, safflower and sunflower oils, corns, vegetable oils are considered omega-6 fatty acid.
