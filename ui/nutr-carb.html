Carbohydrates


Carbohydrates typically make up anywhere from 30-70% of dog food. Carbohydrates gives your dog enough energy to play and run around. These essential ingredients provide your dog with several important funtions such as providing energy, create structure and texture.

  • Carbohydrates from whole grains keep your dog feeling fuller longer and blood sugar steady.
  • Look for words such as whole grain in the ingredients list when purchasing dog food.
  • Quality ingredients will include "whole" in the name. Common sources will be listed in a bag of dog food such as barley, oats, brown rice, whole wheat, whole corn, potato, millet, and much more.

When carbohydrates are provided in your dog's diet they will provide energy and spare the protein and fat to use as a building blocks for the production and maintenance of body tissue. Carbohydrates are from many sources not just grains but also in fruits and vegetables.
